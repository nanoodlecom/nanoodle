**Body:**

Maker here, so flagging that upfront.

nanoodle (https://nanoodle.com) is a node canvas in your browser: text, image,
video and audio AI models as blocks you wire together. If wiring isn't your thing,
there's a describe-what-you-want box — "add a step that translates the caption to
Spanish" — and it edits the graph for you.

The no-code part I care most about is the *output*: when your workflow works, one
click turns it into a standalone app — a single .html file. Not "hosted on our
platform", not "embed our widget" — a file. Email it, drop it on any static host,
open it from a USB stick in ten years. There's no server and no analytics anywhere
in the chain, so the app is genuinely yours.

Honest tradeoff: it's bring-your-own-key. Building and poking around needs no
signup, but running models goes through your own nano-gpt.com key, pay-per-call
(usually cents). I built it this way so I never host your data — but it does mean
one setup step before the magic moment.

30s clip attached of a graph running and becoming an app. Happy to answer
anything — the single-file constraint shaped every design decision.

## Canned replies (adapt, don't paste verbatim)

- **"How is this different from Zapier/Make?"** — Those orchestrate SaaS APIs on
  their servers. nanoodle runs entirely in your browser and the artifact is a file
  you own; it's closer to "ComfyUI meets exported mini-apps" than to automation.
- **"Why not just use ChatGPT?"** — Chat is one model, one turn. This chains models
  (LLM → image → video → audio) into a repeatable app anyone can rerun with their
  own inputs.
- **"Catch?"** — The BYO-key step. Also it's one person's project, so breadth is
  what nano-gpt's catalog offers (which is a lot: GPT/Claude/Gemini/flux/video/TTS).
- **Someone shares what they built** — amplify it hard; user-built noodles are the
  best content we have. Ask permission to feature it (credited) in the in-app
  examples.
